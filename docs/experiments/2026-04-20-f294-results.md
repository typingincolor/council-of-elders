# Stage 11 results — predictions vs reality

**Run id:** `2026-04-20-f294`
**Companion to:** [`2026-04-20-f294-predictions.md`](2026-04-20-f294-predictions.md) (pre-registered before the run) and [`2026-04-20-f294-diversity-split.md`](2026-04-20-f294-diversity-split.md) (full auto-generated report).
**Written:** 2026-04-20, immediately after the run completed.

## The headline

**Synthesis never beat best-R1 in any of the 32 debates.** Across two judges (gemini-2.5-flash + claude-haiku-4.5), 23 agreed on best_r1, 9 split to "tie", zero agreed on synthesis. Every cell's preference rate is below 0.20.

The 2×2 therefore doesn't answer the question it was designed for (does role diversity substitute for model diversity?) because the tool's own output never actually wins. It answers a bigger question: **the debate+synthesis pipeline, as currently implemented, does not add value over picking the strongest individual R1 answer on this corpus with these judges.**

## Threshold verdicts (against Stage 11 pre-declared thresholds)

- **B − A = −0.063** → within ±0.10. Role diversity alone is a **wash**, slightly negative. Personas did reduce R1 claim-overlap (0.677 → 0.422), so they ARE producing more diverse R1s — they just don't translate to better synthesis.
- **C − A = +0.063** → not decisive (threshold >0.10). Model diversity alone did not meaningfully lift synthesis over the homogeneous baseline on this run.
- **C − B = +0.126** → **inconclusive zone** (0.10 < gap ≤ 0.15). Spec says "expand to larger n before drawing a conclusion on personas-as-substitute" — but see "the bigger finding" below.
- **D − C = 0.000** → **two axes do not compose**. Personas on diverse models add no marginal value. The spec's consequence: "drop the default persona pack".

## Jaccard — the only axis where everything worked as expected

Diversity ordering on R1 claim-overlap was clean:

| | same role | different role |
|---|---|---|
| **same model** | 0.677 | 0.422 |
| **different model** | 0.367 | 0.322 |

Both axes independently reduced R1 overlap, and they stacked: going from A (0.677) to D (0.322) is a 52% reduction. **Diversity-of-reasoning at R1 is real and behaves as expected. It just didn't convert into synthesis quality.**

## What this means

### Both pre-run concerns validated

1. *"Those predictions seem to suggest that nothing matters."* Directionally right — in fact my predictions were too generous. Synthesis loses in every cell, including the best-case D.
2. *"Could our debate format just be rubbish?"* This becomes the leading hypothesis. If synthesis underperforms best-R1 across four different roster configurations, including rosters that produced genuinely diverse R1s, the bottleneck probably isn't upstream (roster choice) — it's in the debate-to-synthesis pipeline itself.

### What the 2×2 did NOT answer

It was designed to settle "do personas substitute for model diversity on synthesis quality?". The answer is moot because synthesis quality is below best-R1 in every cell. Even if personas perfectly substituted for model diversity (B = C), they'd both still be below pick-the-best-R1.

### What the 2×2 DID answer

- Personas and model diversity both independently reduce R1 claim-overlap (confirmed).
- At R1 they stack (confirmed).
- At synthesis they don't stack (confirmed — D = C = 0.188, gap 0.000).
- Personas can actively HURT synthesis on the cheap-model baseline (observed: B < A, gap −0.063).

## Hypotheses for why synthesis loses

In order of plausibility:

**Tier 1 (most likely bottlenecks):**
1. **Convergence pressure.** R3+ pushes elders toward `CONVERGED: yes`. Answers may round off distinctive claims to meet in the middle. R3 posts may be blander than R1s.
2. **Context bloat.** Synthesiser sees R1 + R2 questions + R3+ convergence reasoning — thousands of tokens of debate around hundreds of tokens of actual reasoning. Best-R1 gets one clean answer; synthesiser has more to weigh and more to get distracted by.
3. **The synthesis prompt itself.** The Answer/Why/Disagreements structure is recent, ships under-tested. May be cognitively taxing in a way that degrades the output text vs a free-form answer.

**Tier 2 (contributing but probably not decisive):**
4. **Self-bias.** The synthesiser wrote one of the R1s — primed to favour their own framing, not always picking the strongest take.
5. **Peer-directed `@handle` ritual.** Models spend attention on formatting rather than reasoning in R2+.
6. **Short-prompt penalty.** Corpus is single-paragraph questions. Synthesis has limited room to add value beyond pick-the-best — not much to integrate.

**Tier 3 (fallback):**
7. **Judge bias toward tighter-answer preference.** With two answers to compare, judges may default to rewarding the tighter one (best-R1); synthesis is by construction broader and gets penalised for bloat.

## Concrete next step: ablation

The hypothesis that best addresses this result is the one raised pre-run: the debate format itself may be adding noise rather than value. An ablation isolates that.

Four depth variants, same corpus, same roster (`diff_model` — highest-signal), multi-judge scoring:

1. **Bare best-R1.** No synthesis at all.
2. **R1-only synthesis.** Synthesiser sees three R1 answers, writes the final. No R2, no R3, no convergence. Ensembling-as-synthesis.
3. **R1 + R2 synthesis.** One critique round, then synthesise. Minimal debate.
4. **Full debate synthesis.** Current behaviour. Benchmark.

If R1-only synthesis beats full-debate synthesis, debate rounds are negative-value. 32 debates at diff-model prices × 2 judges: roughly $8-12.

## Code changes to consider

Not yet. The result is clear enough that running the ablation first (to isolate the debate-format hypothesis) is higher-value than touching the code. Once the ablation tells us whether debate rounds help or hurt:

- "Cut R2+ rounds entirely; make R1 + immediate synthesis the default" — if debate hurts.
- "Keep R2+; the synthesis prompt is the bottleneck; rewrite it" — if removing debate rounds doesn't help.
- "Tool is mis-framed; pivot to best-R1 picker with optional R2 for specific shapes" — if neither helps.
